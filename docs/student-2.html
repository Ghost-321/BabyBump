What to expect

What will happen to your body?

The second trimester is when all of the big changes happens to the body. The uterus grows, and may some ache as your ligaments stretch. The skin over your belly and breasts will stretch, which may cause mild itching. And the biggest change which are the stretch marks, which tend to fade over time after pregnancy.

Baby development

This is also the stage where the bump will be more visible. The doctors can figure out the gender of the baby if the parents wanted to know what gender the baby would be, but the baby will move around more which will cause more cramps. Also the baby would grow significantly faster as in the first few weeks it will grow only 3 inches but at the 2nd trimester it will grow around 1 whole foot and will have weight added to it.

Medical appointments and tests

People in the second trimester don't need to make a lot of appointments as though it's the period of pregnancy that is meant to be the easiest, you just need to make an appointment once every 4 weeks until the third trimester. But if you are bleeding a lot and still getting more cramps than usual then it's best to go to your assigned doctor to figure out what is happening to your body.

Key take away

Physical changes

The breasts will grow as well as the belly. The uterus would have to expand around the baby to make sure that the baby will grow into the correct shape as well as the breast so that when the baby is born the baby can have all of the nutrients it needs as well as enough antibiotics to help the baby survive from sickness that the mother had experienced.

Healthcare

When you're in the second trimester you will have to do routine check ups with your doctor and if you are completely healthy you would have to do it for every 4 weeks until the 28th week of pregnancy.Then, every 2 weeks until 36 weeks. Then, once a week until delivery.

When to get help

The biggest signs that you have healthy babies normally move around a lot and weigh a lot more in the second trimester than in the first trimester making the womb more bigger. However, if you are having symptoms, such as heavy bleeding and a lot of period cramping in the second trimester you would have to contact your GP straight away.
